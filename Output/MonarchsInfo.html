Nicholas I (971 Jan. 01 – 1001 Oct. 12)

1000–1001

  • Elected

Nicholas was born around 971.
In 1000, at the age of thirty-one he married sixteen-year-old Avelina, the union lasted year and 41 days, but the union proved childless for Nicholas was incapable of breeding.
In 1000 he ascended the throne at the age of thirty-one and reigned the next year and 41 days. Nicholas I was the only ruler from Greater dynasty.

Solomon I (971 Jan. 01 – 1014 Dec. 19)

1001–1014

  • Elected

Solomon was born around 971.
In 992, at the age of twenty-two he married sixteen-year-old Joan, the union lasted 11 years and 356 days, which resulted in two sons and two daughters; their first child, Joan, 1st Princess was born in 993, the surviving children were: Joan, Mabel, and Stephen I. In 1012, at the age of forty-four he married twelve-year-old Edith Quedgeley, second daughter of John Quedgeley, the union lasted two years and 74 days, but the union proved childless for Solomon failed to impregnate his wife.
In 1001 he ascended the throne at the age of thirty-two and reigned the next 14 years and 37 days. Solomon I was the founder of Stainforth dynasty.

Stephen I (1003 Aug. 14 – 1031 May. 24)

1014–1031

  • Son of Solomon I

Stephen was born in 1003 Aug. 14 as the youngest son of thirty-four-year-old Solomon I and twenty-eight-year-old Joan. At the time he had a brother, Solomon.
In 1016, at the age of fourteen he married his cousin twelve-year-old Edith Minchinhampton, second daughter of Osmond Minchinhampton, the union lasted 16 years and 101 days, which resulted in son and daughter; their first child, Solomon II was born in 1028, the surviving children were: Solomon II and Joan.
In 1014 he ascended the throne at the age of eleven and reigned the next 18 years and 155 days. Stephen I was the 2nd ruler from Stainforth dynasty.

Stephen II (1056 Apr. 25 – 1087 Apr. 03)

1081–1087

  • Son of Joan

Stephen was born in 1056 Apr. 25 as the second son of twenty-eight-year-old Osmond Minchinhampton, youngest son of Robert Minchinhampton and twenty-eight-year-old Joan, only daughter of Stephen I.
In 1069, at the age of fourteen he married his second cousin twenty-year-old Mabel Tullibardine, fourth daughter of William Tullibardine, the union lasted three years and 84 days, but the union proved childless for Stephen was incapable of breeding. In 1072, at the age of seventeen he married his second cousin fourteen-year-old Alviva Tullibardine, seventh daughter of William Tullibardine, the union lasted 16 years and 92 days, but the union proved childless for both the husband and the wife appeared incapable of reproducing.
In 1081 he ascended the throne at the age of twenty-seven and reigned the next six years and 160 days. Stephen II was the only ruler from Minchinhampton dynasty.

In 1091, at the age of eighteen he married thirty-six-year-old Emma Stalbridge, firstborn daughter of Geoffrey Stalbridge, the union lasted seven years and 203 days, but the union proved childless for Emma was barren.
In 1087 he ascended the throne at the age of fourteen and reigned the next 11 years and 335 days. Arthur was the founder of long-lasting Loddon dynasty.
